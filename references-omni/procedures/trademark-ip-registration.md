## PART 1 — TRADEMARK REGISTRATION

### Why Register a Trademark in Nepal?

- Unregistered marks have limited protection — registration gives exclusive right to use
- Required for e-commerce platform verification (E-Commerce Act 2081)
- Protection against counterfeiting and passing off
- Basis for Customs recordal to stop infringing imports
- Required for franchise and licensing agreements

### Step 1 — Trademark Search (Pre-Application)

**Before filing, search for conflicting marks:**
- Search at DOI Intellectual Property Section (Kathmandu)
- Online search portal: verify availability at doi.gov.np (confirm if online search is available)
- Search for: identical marks, phonetically similar marks, visually similar marks in the same class

**Nepal follows the Nice Classification system (45 classes):**

| Class Range | Category |
|------------|---------|
| 1–34 | Goods |
| 35–45 | Services |

*Identify which class(es) your goods/services fall under before filing.*

### Step 2 — Prepare Application

**Documents Required:**

| Document | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Trademark application form | DOI-prescribed form |
| Representation of the mark | Clear image: minimum [5×5 cm], colour or B&W |
| List of goods/services (with class numbers) | Per Nice Classification |
| Citizenship certificate / OCR registration | Certified copy |
| Power of Attorney | If filed through an IP agent/attorney |
| Priority document | If claiming convention priority (Paris Convention — verify Nepal's membership status) |
| Translation | If mark contains foreign words — Nepali translation required |

**Application Fee:** Per class (verify current fee schedule at doi.gov.np)

### Step 3 — Filing & Examination

**Authority:** DOI, Intellectual Property Section, Kathmandu (Tripureshwor or current office — verify)

**Examination Process:**
1. Formal examination — completeness of documents
2. Substantive examination — distinctiveness, conflicts with existing marks
3. If objected: applicant receives examination report and must respond within [VERIFY DEADLINE]
4. If accepted: published in the Official Gazette (Rajpatra) for opposition period

**Opposition Period:** [90 days] from gazette publication — any person may oppose registration

### Step 4 — Registration

If no opposition (or opposition resolved in applicant's favor):
- Certificate of Registration issued
- **Protection period:** 7 years from registration date
- **Renewal:** Every 7 years — file renewal application before expiry

### Trademark Enforcement

| Action | Forum | Remedy |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Civil infringement suit | District Court | Injunction, damages |
| Criminal complaint | Police / Court | Imprisonment + fine under Patent, Design and Trademark Act |
| Customs recordal | Department of Customs | Border seizure of infringing goods |
| Domain name dispute | ICANN / .np registry | Dispute resolution |

## PART 2 — PATENT REGISTRATION

### What is Patentable in Nepal?

- Novel invention (not previously disclosed anywhere in the world)
- Involves an inventive step (non-obvious)
- Has industrial applicability
- **Not patentable:** Scientific theories, mathematical methods, business methods (as such), mental acts, computer programs (as such — verify current position)

### Patent Application Process

**Step 1 — Prior Art Search**
Search existing patents globally (Google Patents, WIPO PatentScope) to confirm novelty before filing.

**Step 2 — File Application at DOI**

**Documents Required:**
- Patent application form
- Complete specification (description of invention, claims, abstract)
- Drawings / diagrams (if applicable)
- Inventor's declaration
- Assignment deed (if applicant ≠ inventor)
- Citizenship / company registration documents
- Priority document (if international priority claimed)

**Step 3 — Examination & Grant**
- Formal examination
- Substantive examination (technical novelty assessment)
- Publication in Rajpatra
- Grant of patent certificate

**Protection Period:** 7 years from registration, renewable up to 20 years total (verify current Nepal term)

## PART 3 — COPYRIGHT REGISTRATION

### Automatic Protection

Copyright in Nepal arises **automatically** upon creation of an original work — registration is not mandatory for protection. However, registration creates:
- Prima facie evidence of ownership
- Public record of the work
- Stronger position in enforcement

### Works Protected

- Literary works (books, articles, software source code)
- Musical works and sound recordings
- Artistic works (paintings, photographs, illustrations)
- Cinematographic works
- Dramatic works
- Computer programs and databases

**Protection Period:** Author's lifetime + 50 years (verify current period at lawcommission.gov.np)

### Copyright Registration Process

**Authority:** Nepal Copyright Registrar (under Ministry of Education / verify current ministry)

**Documents:**
- Application form
- Copy of the work
- Author/owner citizenship certificate
- Statement of originality

**Fee:** Verify current fee at the Copyright Registrar's office

### Software / Tech Company Copyright Note

Source code is protected as a literary work under Copyright Act 2059. For SaaS / tech companies:
- Register core software modules
- Maintain version-controlled code repositories as evidence of creation date
- Use copyright notices: © [YEAR] [COMPANY NAME]. All Rights Reserved.

## PART 4 — INDUSTRIAL DESIGN REGISTRATION

Protects the **aesthetic / visual features** of a product (shape, pattern, colour).

**Requirements:** Novel, original, not dictated purely by function
**Protection Period:** 5 years, renewable (verify current term at doi.gov.np)
**Process:** Similar to trademark — file at DOI with representations of the design
