---

## OVERVIEW

Nepal is one of the world's largest labor-exporting countries. The **Foreign Employment Act 2064** regulates:
- Recruitment agencies (Manpower Companies)
- Workers going abroad for employment
- Employers / sponsors of Nepali workers overseas
- Pre-departure requirements and worker protections

**Key principle:** Working abroad without proper documentation = illegal status, loss of insurance, no legal protection if exploited.

---

## WHO THIS AFFECTS

| Party | Obligation |
|-------|-----------|
| **Recruitment Agencies (Manpower Companies)** | Must be licensed by DoFE; liable for worker welfare |
| **Individual Workers** | Must follow proper documentation process; entitled to protections |
| **Employers (Nepal-based) sending staff overseas** | Must comply with DoFE requirements for staff |
| **Foreign Employers** | Must work through DoFE-licensed agencies or direct approval |

## PART 1 — FOR RECRUITMENT AGENCIES (MANPOWER COMPANIES)

### Licensing Requirements

**Authority:** Department of Foreign Employment (DoFE), Kathmandu

**Eligibility:**
- Company registered under Companies Act 2063
- Minimum capital: **NPR [VERIFY CURRENT REQUIREMENT at dofe.gov.np]**
- No criminal record of directors
- Office space requirement

**License Application Documents:**
- Company registration certificate (OCR)
- PAN certificate
- Tax clearance certificate
- Office lease / ownership proof
- Bank guarantee / security deposit: **NPR [VERIFY CURRENT AMOUNT]**
- Directors' citizenship certificates and police clearance
- DoFE application form + fee

**License Validity:** [Verify current renewal period at dofe.gov.np]

### Agency Obligations

Licensed agencies **must:**
- Recruit workers only for approved destination countries and job categories
- Provide orientation training (pre-departure training) to all workers
- Ensure workers have adequate insurance coverage
- Not charge workers more than the DoFE-approved service fee — **overcharging is a criminal offense**
- Maintain records of all workers placed
- Assist workers in distress abroad
- Report worker deaths, serious injuries, and exploitation cases to DoFE

**Prohibited:**
- Recruiting for banned destination countries (verify current banned list at dofe.gov.np)
- Recruiting for banned job categories (domestic workers in certain countries)
- Charging workers for services beyond the DoFE-approved ceiling
- Misrepresenting job conditions, salary, or employer details

## PART 2 — FOR INDIVIDUAL WORKERS GOING ABROAD

### Step 1 — Verify Destination Country and Job Category

**Approved destinations:** DoFE maintains a list of approved destination countries. Working in an **unapproved country** means:
- No DoFE protection
- No mandatory insurance
- No legal recourse through DoFE

**Live-verify approved countries at: dofe.gov.np**

### Step 2 — Choose a Licensed Recruitment Agency

Only use DoFE-licensed manpower agencies. Verify license status at: dofe.gov.np → Licensed Agencies

**Do not pay fees to unlicensed agents — this is trafficking risk territory.**

### Step 3 — Labor Agreement / Appointment Letter

Before departing, obtain and verify:
- Appointment letter from the foreign employer (specifying job title, salary, working hours, accommodation)
- Labor agreement / employment contract in a language you understand
- Have the contract verified by the licensed agency

### Step 4 — Pre-Departure Orientation

**Mandatory pre-departure orientation training** at a DoFE-approved training center:
- Duration: [VERIFY CURRENT REQUIREMENT — typically 1–3 days]
- Topics: Rights abroad, emergency contacts, banking, basic language, safety
- Certificate issued on completion — required for labor permit

### Step 5 — Labor Permit (श्रम स्वीकृति — Shram Swikruti)

**Authority:** Department of Foreign Employment (DoFE)

**Required to obtain Labor Permit:**
- Application form
- Appointment letter / employment contract (verified)
- Passport (valid for at least 6 months beyond contract period)
- Pre-departure orientation certificate
- Insurance premium payment receipt
- Agency's recommendation
- DoFE fee payment (verify current fee at dofe.gov.np)

**Processing Time:** [Verify — typically a few days if documents complete]

**Individual (Free Visa, Free Ticket) route:** Some workers may go independently (not through an agency) — verify current free visa/free ticket country list at dofe.gov.np.

### Step 6 — Mandatory Insurance

**Authority:** Foreign Employment Promotion Board (FEPB) — insurance scheme

All Nepali workers going abroad must have **Foreign Employment Insurance**:
- Premium: **NPR [VERIFY CURRENT PREMIUM at dofe.gov.np]**
- Coverage: Death, disability, medical treatment, repatriation of body
- Paid before departure — receipt required for labor permit

### Step 7 — Departure

After receiving labor permit:
- Depart through Tribhuvan International Airport (Kathmandu) or authorized entry points
- Carry: Passport, labor permit, employment contract, insurance certificate, employer/agency contact details, Nepal Embassy contact in destination country

## PART 3 — WORKER PROTECTIONS & RIGHTS ABROAD

| Right | Enforcement |
|-------|------------|
| Salary as per contract | Report to Nepali Embassy if withheld |
| Safe working conditions | Report to Nepali Embassy + DoFE |
| Right to return home | Employer must provide return ticket in most agreements |
| Insurance claim (death/disability) | File through FEPB insurance scheme |
| Repatriation of body | FEPB insurance + Nepali Embassy assistance |
| Emergency assistance | Nepal Embassy in destination country |

**Key Contacts:**
- DoFE Helpline: [Verify at dofe.gov.np]
- FEPB: [fepb.gov.np]

## PART 4 — BANNED DESTINATIONS & JOB CATEGORIES

DoFE periodically bans certain destinations or job categories when exploitation risk is high.

**Current bans:** Live-verify at dofe.gov.np — this list changes frequently.

Common historic bans: Domestic work in certain Gulf countries, some Middle East destinations at various times.

Working in a banned country/job category:
- Worker loses DoFE protection
- No insurance coverage
- Criminal liability may fall on the agency
- Worker may face deportation from destination country

## PART 5 — ILLEGAL RECRUITMENT (HUMAN TRAFFICKING RISK)

**Warning signs of illegal recruitment:**
- Agent not DoFE-licensed
- No appointment letter / vague job description
- Request to pay large fees upfront
- Passport taken by agent before departure
- Visa category doesn't match job (tourist visa for work)
- No pre-departure orientation or insurance

**If exploitation is suspected:**
- Contact Nepal Embassy in destination country immediately
- Contact DoFE: dofe.gov.np / helpline
- Report to National Human Rights Commission (nhrc.org.np)
- Contact the Attorney General's office on return

**Human Trafficking and Transportation Control Act 2064** provides criminal penalties for traffickers and civil remedies for victims.
