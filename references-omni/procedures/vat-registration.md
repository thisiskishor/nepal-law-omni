# VAT Registration in Nepal — Step-by-Step Guide
# Governing Law: Value Added Tax Act 2052 (1995 AD); VAT Rules 2053
#                Income Tax Act 2058 (for linked PAN requirements)
# Authority: Inland Revenue Department (IRD) — ird.gov.np
# Live-verify current thresholds and rates at: ird.gov.np | rajpatra.dop.gov.np

---

## VAT REGISTRATION THRESHOLD (Live-Verify Before Advising)

| Registration Type | Annual Turnover Threshold | Obligation |
|-----------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| **Mandatory** | Exceeds **NPR 50 lakh (5,000,000)** | Must register — penalty for non-registration |
| **Voluntary** | Below NPR 50 lakh | Optional — useful for B2B businesses to claim input VAT |
| **Mandatory (specific sectors)** | Any turnover — verify sector list | Certain professions and sectors must register regardless of turnover |

> **Live-verify the current threshold at ird.gov.np — the Finance Act updates this annually.**

## CURRENT VAT RATE

| Supply Type | VAT Rate |
|------------|---------|
| Standard rate (most goods and services) | **13%** |
| Zero-rated (exports, certain essential goods) | **0%** |
| Exempt (medical, education, certain agricultural) | **Exempt — no VAT, no input credit** |

> **Live-verify the current rate and exempt/zero-rated list at ird.gov.np — the Finance Act can amend these.**

---

## WHO MUST REGISTER

**Mandatory registration applies to:**
- Businesses with annual turnover exceeding NPR 50 lakh
- Importers (regardless of turnover)
- Certain specified professions (verify current list at IRD)
- E-commerce platforms with Nepal-based revenue (verify under E-Commerce Act 2081)
- Foreign digital service providers serving Nepal consumers (verify current position)

**Voluntary registration is useful when:**
- You supply to VAT-registered businesses (they can claim input VAT credit)
- You export goods/services (zero-rated — claim input VAT refund)
- You want to appear credible/formal to business customers

## STEP-BY-STEP REGISTRATION PROCESS

### Step 1 — PAN Registration (If Not Done)

VAT registration requires a valid PAN. If PAN is not yet obtained:
1. Apply at IRD or online at ird.gov.np
2. Submit: citizenship certificate, OCR certificate, office address proof
3. PAN certificate issued within [1–3] working days

---

### Step 2 — Prepare VAT Registration Documents

| Document | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| VAT registration application form | IRD-prescribed form (obtain at IRD or ird.gov.np) |
| PAN certificate | Certified copy |
| Business registration certificate (OCR) | Certified copy |
| MoA / AoA (if company) | Certified copy |
| Citizenship certificate of owner / authorized rep | Certified copy |
| Office lease agreement / ownership proof | Proof of business premises |
| Bank account details | Account number, bank name, branch |
| Most recent income tax return (if available) | For established businesses |

---

### Step 3 — Submit Application

**Authority:** Inland Revenue Department (IRD), Large or Medium Taxpayer Office, or District Inland Revenue Office nearest to your principal business address

**Online filing:** Check ird.gov.np for current online VAT registration availability — Nepal has been expanding e-services

**In-person:** Submit at the relevant IRD office counter

**Registration Fee:** [Verify at IRD — typically nil or nominal]

---

### Step 4 — IRD Verification

IRD may:
- Conduct a physical verification of the business premises
- Request additional documents
- Interview the applicant

**Timeline:** VAT certificate typically issued within **[3–7] working days** of complete application submission (verify current processing time at IRD)

---

### Step 5 — Receive VAT Registration Certificate

**Output:** VAT Registration Certificate with a **VAT Registration Number**

This number must be displayed on:
- All VAT invoices
- Business letterhead (recommended)
- Platform / website (if e-commerce)

## POST-REGISTRATION OBLIGATIONS

### VAT Invoice Requirements

Every taxable supply must be accompanied by a **VAT invoice** containing:
- Seller's name and VAT number
- Buyer's name and address (and VAT number if VAT-registered)
- Invoice date and unique invoice number
- Description of goods/services
- Quantity, unit price, and total
- VAT amount (13%) separately stated
- Total amount including VAT

**IRD-Prescribed Invoice Format:** Nepal uses a standardized VAT invoice format — obtain from IRD or use IRD-approved accounting software.

### VAT Return Filing

| Period | Filing Deadline |
|--------|---------------|
| Monthly VAT return | By the **25th** of the following month |
| Annual reconciliation | [Verify current deadline at IRD] |

**Online filing:** Via ird.gov.np TaxPayer Portal (mandatory for most taxpayers — verify current requirement)

### VAT Payment

VAT collected from customers minus input VAT credit on purchases = **Net VAT payable to IRD**

Payment deadline: Same as return filing deadline — 25th of following month

**Late payment:** Interest and penalty apply — verify current rates at ird.gov.np

### VAT Audit

IRD may conduct VAT audits. Maintain:
- All VAT invoices (issued and received) for **[5 years]** minimum
- Purchase records and input VAT credit claims
- Bank statements
- Accounting records reconciling VAT returns

---

## INPUT VAT CREDIT RULES

VAT paid on business purchases can be **offset against VAT collected on sales**:

| Situation | Input VAT Credit |
|-----------|----------------|
| Purchase for taxable supplies | **Claimable in full** |
| Purchase for exempt supplies | **Not claimable** |
| Mixed use (taxable + exempt) | **Claimable pro-rata** |
| Capital goods (above threshold) | **Spread over useful life or claimable upfront — verify current rule** |

---

## VAT DEREGISTRATION

If turnover drops below threshold or business closes:
- Apply for deregistration at IRD
- File final VAT return
- Pay all outstanding VAT
- Return VAT certificate
- IRD may conduct a final audit before deregistration

---

## E-COMMERCE & DIGITAL SERVICES VAT NOTE

- Nepal-based e-commerce platforms collecting from Nepal consumers: **VAT applies**
- Foreign digital service providers (streaming, SaaS) serving Nepal consumers: **Verify current position** — IRD has been developing rules for foreign digital services VAT — live-check at ird.gov.np
