# Industrial Enterprise Registration in Nepal — Step-by-Step Guide
# Governing Law: Industrial Enterprises Act 2076 (2019 AD)
#                Foreign Investment and Technology Transfer Act (FITTA) 2075 (2019 AD)
# Authority: Department of Industry (DOI) — doi.gov.np
# Live-verify at: doi.gov.np | lawcommission.gov.np | rajpatra.dop.gov.np

---

## Why This Is Separate from Company Registration

**Company registration (OCR)** creates the legal entity.
**Industrial registration (DOI)** creates the operating license for manufacturing, processing, or industry-defined enterprises.

Both are required for industrial businesses. Sequence: Company formation first → then Industry registration.

## Pre-Registration Requirements

- [ ] Company incorporated at OCR (Certificate of Incorporation in hand)
- [ ] PAN obtained from IRD
- [ ] Land/building for factory: ownership deed or lease agreement
- [ ] Project/Business Plan (for medium/large industries)
- [ ] Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) or Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) — required for industries above threshold (verify current thresholds at mofe.gov.np)

---

## Step 1 — Industry Type Determination & Negative List Check

Identify under which classification your industry falls and verify:
1. Not on the **Negative List** (industries prohibited in Nepal)
2. Not on the **Protected List** (if FDI involved)
3. If special licensing is needed (e.g., pharmaceutical, arms, explosives, mining)

**Live-verify current lists:** doi.gov.np → Industry Registration → Negative/Protected List

---

## Step 2 — Environmental Clearance (if applicable)

**Authority:** Ministry of Forests and Environment / relevant Provincial Ministry
**Applicability:**
- Industries above specified pollution/impact thresholds must obtain IEE or EIA clearance before registration
- EIA is mandatory for large industries and certain hazardous processes

**Timeline:** 3–12 months (EIA can take significantly longer — plan accordingly)

---

## Step 3 — Submit Industry Registration Application

**Authority:** Department of Industry (DOI), Kathmandu [or Provincial Industry Office for provincial industries]
**Portal:** Online via doi.gov.np (verify current online system availability)

**Documents Required:**

| Document | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Application form | DOI-prescribed form |
| Company Registration Certificate | From OCR |
| MoA / AoA | Certified copies |
| PAN Certificate | |
| Citizenship certificates of all promoters | Certified copies |
| Land ownership deed / lease agreement for premises | |
| Project report / Business plan | Required for medium/large |
| Environmental clearance | If applicable |
| Foreign investment approval (if FDI) | FITTA approval letter from DOI |

**Registration Fee:** Based on fixed capital (sliding scale — verify current rates at doi.gov.np)

**Timeline:** [15–30] working days for small/medium; longer for large or EIA-dependent

---

## Step 4 — FDI Approval (Foreign Investment Only — FITTA 2075)

If any promoter is a foreign national or foreign entity:

1. Submit FDI application to Department of Industry (DOI)
2. DOI reviews against Negative/Protected Lists and FITTA criteria
3. Approval letter issued — specifies:
   - Approved investment amount (minimum USD 50,000 — verify current threshold)
   - Approved business activities
   - Repatriation entitlements
4. NRB approval for forex inflows — must be obtained separately (nrb.org.np)

**Timeline:** [30–60] working days for FDI approval

## Step 5 — Obtain Industry Registration Certificate

**Output:** Industry Registration Certificate from DOI

This certificate must be renewed [annually / as specified — verify current renewal cycle at doi.gov.np].

## Prohibited Industries (Negative List — Verify Current List at doi.gov.np)

Industries typically on the Negative List (verify — this changes by regulation):
- Arms, ammunition, explosives (except for specific government licensees)
- Nuclear/radioactive materials
- Foreign ownership in small cottage industries
- Certain agricultural activities reserved for Nepali nationals

> **Always live-verify the current Negative and Protected Lists before advising any client on industry feasibility.**
