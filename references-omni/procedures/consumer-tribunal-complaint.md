# Consumer Tribunal Complaint — Filing Guide
# Governing Law: Consumer Protection Act 2075 (2018 AD) & Regulation 2076
#                E-Commerce Act 2081 (for e-commerce platform disputes)
# Authority: Consumer Tribunal (उपभोक्ता न्यायाधिकरण) — under Department of Commerce
# Live-verify at: doc.gov.np | lawcommission.gov.np | rajpatra.dop.gov.np

---

## WHEN TO USE THE CONSUMER TRIBUNAL

The Consumer Tribunal is a specialized quasi-judicial body for consumer disputes. Use it when:
- A seller / platform has sold defective, substandard, or misdescribed goods
- A platform refuses to honor the mandatory 7-day return under the E-Commerce Act 2081
- A seller has used misleading pricing or deceptive trade practices
- A service provider has provided below-standard service
- A platform has failed to respond to a consumer complaint within the statutory period

**Advantage over District Court:** Faster, cheaper, specialized — designed specifically for consumer disputes.

---

## CONSUMER RIGHTS UNDER CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT 2075

| Right | Provision |
|-------|----------|
| Right to safety | Protection from hazardous goods/services |
| Right to information | Accurate product/pricing information |
| Right to choice | No monopolistic forcing of goods |
| Right to be heard | Complaint redressal mechanism |
| Right to redress | Compensation for defective goods/services |
| Right to fair treatment | No discrimination in consumer transactions |

## STEP-BY-STEP FILING PROCESS

### Step 1 — Exhaust Internal Grievance First

Before filing with the Tribunal, you must:
1. Contact the seller / platform's grievance mechanism
2. Wait for their response (typically [7–14] days)
3. If unsatisfied or no response — proceed to Tribunal

*Keep all evidence of your complaint attempts — emails, chat logs, receipts.*

---

### Step 2 — Prepare Your Complaint

**Complaint must include:**

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Complainant details | Full name, citizenship number, address, phone |
| Respondent details | Seller / platform name, address, DoC Listing No. (if platform) |
| Transaction details | Date of purchase, order number, amount paid, product/service |
| Nature of complaint | Defective product / refund refused / misleading description / other |
| Relief sought | Replacement / refund / compensation / penalty |
| Evidence | Receipts, photos, screenshots, chat logs, return request records |

### Step 4 — Submit the Complaint

**Where to submit:**
- Consumer Tribunal in your district / province
- Department of Commerce online portal (verify if available at doc.gov.np)

**Filing fee:** Verify current fee at the Tribunal — typically low for consumer cases

**Documents to bring (originals + copies):**
- Complaint letter
- Your citizenship certificate
- All annexures
- Filing fee receipt

## E-COMMERCE SPECIFIC COMPLAINTS (E-Commerce Act 2081)

For platforms registered under the E-Commerce Act 2081, additional escalation path:

1. File complaint on **doc.gov.np** platform portal
2. DoCSCP may conduct investigation directly
3. Platform may be fined up to NPR 500,000 for systematic violations
4. Platform's DoC listing may be suspended or revoked
