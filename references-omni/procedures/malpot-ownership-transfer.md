# Land Ownership Transfer at the Malpot Office — Complete Guide
# Governing Law: Land Revenue Act 2034 (1978 AD); Land Act 2077 (2021 AD)
# Authority: District Land Revenue Office (मालपोत कार्यालय — Malpot Karyalaya)
# MANDATORY: Physical presence with biometrics. Cannot be completed online.
# Live-verify at: mlmpa.gov.np | rajpatra.dop.gov.np

---

## Overview

The **Malpot Office** (Land Revenue Office) is the exclusive authority for registering land ownership transfers in Nepal. No sale, gift, inheritance partition, or mortgage of land is legally valid until registered here.

**Principle:** Ownership of land in Nepal is established by the **Lalpurja** (लालपुर्जा — Title Certificate). No possession, agreement, or payment alone creates title. Registration at the Malpot is the final step that creates legal ownership.

## Pre-Transfer Checklist

Before appearing at the Malpot Office, complete all of these:

### 1. Verify Current Ownership
- Obtain a **Certified Copy of Lalpurja** from the Malpot Office
- Verify that the title is in the name of the Seller / Transferor
- Check for any previous encumbrances, mortgages, or registered disputes

### 2. Obtain Encumbrance Certificate (दाखिल खारेज — Dakhil Kharej)
- Issued by the Malpot Office on request
- Confirms whether any mortgage, lien, court order, or dispute is registered against the land
- **Do not proceed if encumbrance exists without clearing it first**

### 3. Obtain Tax Clearance
- Land Revenue (Malpot) arrears — must be nil
- Local government property tax — must be nil (obtain clearance from VDC/Municipality)

### 4. Verify Land Ceiling Compliance (Land Act 2077)
- Check that the Buyer's total landholding post-transfer does not exceed permitted ceiling
- Ceilings vary by region (Terai / Hilly / Mountain) and family size
- **Live-verify current ceiling limits** — Land Act 2077 has been amended (Some Nepal Acts Amendment Ordinance 2083 may apply)

### 5. Check Land Classification
- Certain land classifications have transfer restrictions:
  - **Guthi land:** Restricted — special process required
  - **Government / public land:** Cannot be transferred
  - **Forest / watershed:** Restricted
  - **Agricultural land with pre-emption:** Neighboring farmers may have first right of purchase in certain conditions (verify)

### 6. Obtain Minimum Value Certificate
- The Malpot Office publishes annual **Minimum Valuation Rates** (न्यूनतम मूल्य)
- Stamp duty is calculated on the **higher** of: agreed sale price OR government minimum value
- Obtain the current minimum valuation for the specific plot before finalizing sale price

## Step-by-Step Transfer Procedure (Sale)

### Step 1 — Prepare the Sale Deed

Draft the Sale Deed (Rajinama) — use `references-omni/templates/land-sale-deed.md`.

The deed must contain:
- Full names, citizenship numbers, addresses of both parties
- Exact property description: Kitta No., Survey Sheet No., Lalpurja No., area, classification, boundaries
- Agreed price and confirmation of receipt
- Warranty clauses from Seller
- Witness details

---

### Step 2 — Appear at the Malpot Office

**Both parties must appear in person.** No online filing or remote execution for standard transfers.

**Documents Required (bring originals + certified copies):**

| Document | Seller | Buyer |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Original Citizenship Certificate | ✓ | ✓ |
| Original Lalpurja (Title Certificate) | ✓ | — |
| Encumbrance Certificate (current, within 35 days) | ✓ | — |
| Malpot tax clearance | ✓ | — |
| Local government tax clearance | ✓ | — |
| Napi Naksha (Survey Map / Kitta Map) | ✓ | — |
| Relationship certificate (for family transfers) | If applicable | — |
| Land ceiling self-declaration | — | ✓ |
| Citizenship of witnesses (2 witnesses) | ✓ | ✓ |

---

### Step 3 — Deed Reading & Execution

At the Malpot Office:
1. Both parties appear before the Land Revenue Officer
2. The deed is read aloud and confirmed by both parties
3. Signatures or thumbprints of both parties and two witnesses are affixed
4. The Land Revenue Officer verifies identities (biometric / fingerprint scan may be required)

---

### Step 4 — Pay Stamp Duty & Registration Fee

**Stamp Duty (दस्तुर) — Current Rates (VERIFY at Malpot Office — rates change with Finance Act):**

| Relationship / Category | Stamp Duty Rate |
|------------------------|----------------|
| General / third-party sale | ~4–5% of property value |
| Transfer to direct family (spouse, children) | Reduced rate (verify current) |
| Transfer to daughter / female buyer | Reduced / concessional rate (verify — Finance Act updates) |
| First-time homebuyer (affordable housing) | Check for any exemptions |

**Calculation base:** Higher of agreed price or government minimum valuation.

**Payment:** At designated bank / IRD counter at or near the Malpot Office. Bring payment receipt.

---

### Step 5 — Registration & Issuance of New Lalpurja

The Land Revenue Officer:
1. Records the transfer in the land register
2. Cancels the Seller's Lalpurja
3. Issues a new Lalpurja in the Buyer's name

**Timeline for registration:** Same day or 1–3 working days, depending on office workload and completeness of documents.

**Registration must be completed within 35 days of deed execution** — late registration attracts penalties.

## Special Transfer Situations

### Gift Transfer (Daanpatra / दानपत्र)
- Reduced stamp duty for gifts to close family members (verify current rates)
- Must be voluntary and registered
- Donor cannot revoke a registered gift except for specific statutory grounds

### Inheritance / Partition (Ansa-Banda / अंशबण्डा)
- All co-heirs must sign or issue Power of Attorney for absent heirs
- May require court order if any heir is minor or disputes the partition
- Stamp duty: lower rates for inheritance (verify current)

### Mortgage (Dharni Bandhak / धर्नी बन्धक)
- Mortgage must be registered at Malpot to be valid against third parties
- Unregistered mortgage: enforceable between parties but may lose priority
- On loan repayment: lender must issue a Mortgage Release (Fukaawa) — register at Malpot

### Power of Attorney Transfers
- PoA holder may execute deed on behalf of absent party
- PoA must be notarized and verified as current/not revoked before the Malpot Officer accepts it

## Common Rejection Reasons — Avoid These

| Problem | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| Encumbrance found | Obtain and clear encumbrance certificate before proceeding |
| Tax arrears outstanding | Pay all Malpot and local tax before appearing |
| Lalpurja name doesn't match Seller | Verify ownership carefully; initiate correction first |
| Ceiling violation | Check Buyer's current holdings; obtain DOL clearance if needed |
| Incomplete deed (missing Kitta No., boundaries, etc.) | Use the full deed template; verify all field values |
| Missing witness | Bring two witnesses with original citizenship documents |
