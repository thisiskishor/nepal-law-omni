## CRITICAL OVERVIEW: ADOPTION IN NEPAL

Adoption in Nepal is a **court-supervised process** governed primarily by the **Child Rights Act 2075** and the **Muluki Civil Code 2074**. A private adoption deed without court approval is **legally invalid** and unenforceable.

### Types of Adoption in Nepal

| Type | Process | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Domestic Adoption** | Nepal citizen adopting a Nepal child | District Court + Child Welfare Board |
| **International Adoption** | Foreign national adopting Nepal child | Nepal has **suspended** international adoption — verify current status at ccwb.gov.np |
| **Relative Adoption** | Adopting a relative's child | Simplified process — court approval still required |

> **International Adoption Suspension:** Nepal has periodically suspended international adoptions. **Live-verify current status at ccwb.gov.np before advising any foreign national client.** Advising on international adoption without checking current status could expose clients to serious legal risk.

## STEP-BY-STEP ADOPTION PROCESS (DOMESTIC)

### Step 1 — Application to District Child Welfare Board

**Authority:** District Child Welfare Board (DCWB) of the district where the child resides

**Documents Required:**
- Application for adoption (DCWB-prescribed form)
- Citizenship certificates of both prospective parents (certified)
- Marriage certificate of prospective parents
- Income proof (tax return, salary certificate, bank statements)
- Property ownership / lease documents (proof of stable residence)
- Health certificates (prospective parents and their biological children if any)
- Police clearance certificate (no criminal record)
- Character reference letters ([2] respected community members)
- Photographs of prospective parents and home

**DCWB Process:**
1. Application review and home study
2. Social worker visit to assess home environment
3. DCWB recommendation report

### Step 2 — Match with Child

DCWB identifies a suitable child from:
- Government children's homes
- Licensed child care organizations
- Relinquishment cases

### Step 3 — Trial Period (Pre-Adoption Placement)

Child lives with prospective adoptive parents for a **trial period** [verify current duration — typically 3–6 months]. Social worker monitors the placement.

### Step 4 — Court Application

After successful trial period, file adoption petition before the **District Court**:

### Step 5 — Court Hearing & Order

The Court:
- Reviews all documents
- Examines petitioners and may examine the child
- Consults the child's expressed wishes (if child is of sufficient age)
- Issues **Adoption Order** if satisfied all requirements are met

**Timeline:** Varies significantly — 3 months to 1+ year depending on complexity and court backlog
