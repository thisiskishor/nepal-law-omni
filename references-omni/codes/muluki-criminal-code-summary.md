## Overview

The Muluki Criminal Code 2074 replaced the criminal portions of the old Muluki Ain and consolidates Nepal's penal law. It defines offenses, penalties, and criminal liability. The Muluki Criminal Procedure Code 2074 governs how those offenses are investigated, prosecuted, and adjudicated.

**Commencement:** Bhadra 17, 2075 BS (September 1, 2018 AD).

---

## Offense Classification

| Category | Description | Typical Penalty Range |
|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Serious (गम्भीर)** | Murder, rape, organized crime, corruption | 10 years to life imprisonment |
| **Major (ठूलो)** | Fraud, assault causing grievous injury, arson | 3–10 years imprisonment |
| **Minor (साना)** | Petty theft, minor assault, public disorder | Up to 3 years / fine |
| **Administrative (प्रशासनिक)** | Regulatory breaches, documentation offenses | Fine / brief detention |

## Criminal Procedure — Key Concepts

### FIR Filing (जाहेरी — Jaheri)

| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Who can file | Victim, witness, or any person with knowledge |
| Where to file | Nearest Police Station (थाना) or District Police Office |
| Form | Written or oral (reduced to writing by officer) |
| Time limit | Varies by offense — some offenses have limitation periods; FIR should be filed promptly |

**FIR Refusal:** If police refuse to register, complainant may approach the District Court directly.

### Arrest

| Type | Warrant Required? | Grounds |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| With warrant (Purji/पुर्जी) | Yes | Court-issued on police application |
| Without warrant | No | Caught in the act (रंगेहात / Rangahat) or reasonable suspicion of serious offense |

### Remand & Bail

| Concept | Nepali | Rule |
|---------|--------|------|
| Remand / Custody | ठुना (Thuna) | Court-ordered; initial period [25 days] for serious offenses — verify |
| Bail | धरौटी (Dharauti) | Court discretion; denied for serious non-bailable offenses |
| Bail conditions | — | Surety, passport surrender, periodic reporting |
| Anticipatory bail | — | Not expressly codified; seek Supreme Court writ in urgent cases |

## Defenses

| Defense | Application |
|---------|------------|
| Self-defense | Proportionate force to defend person or property — no criminal liability |
| Consent | For offenses where consent negates element (not applicable to serious crimes) |
| Insanity | Total lack of mental capacity at time of offense |
| Minority | Under 10: no criminal liability; 10–14: reduced liability; 14–18: juvenile procedure |
| Mistake of fact | Genuine and reasonable mistake negating criminal intent |
